Peptides are short chains of amino acids — the building blocks of proteins — that act as precise signaling molecules in the body. They communicate with cells to trigger specific biological responses. Therapeutic peptides have excellent safety profiles because they mimic your body's own processes. When used under licensed medical supervision with proper dosing and pharmaceutical-grade sourcing, they are considered very safe.

Most therapeutic peptides are administered via subcutaneous injection — a small needle just under the skin, similar to a diabetic insulin injection. It sounds intimidating but is virtually painless. Some peptides are available as nasal sprays, topical creams, or oral tablets. Your protocol will include full administration instructions and our team is available to walk you through it.
